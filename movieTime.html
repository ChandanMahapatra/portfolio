Movie-Time

Ambient Display

Take a look

Overview

Movie-time is an ambient display that can help people to remember to go to the theatres more often. It projects an image that losely resembles movie genres, so that people might get interested in going to the theatre after getting notified about a popular movie genre

My Role

Hardware Prototyping, Product Design and Development

Team

Chandan and Valerie

Tools

Adobe Illustrator, Brackets, LulzBot Mini 3D Printer, Particle Photon Kit, Tinkercad and VersaLASER Laser Cutter

Problem

Make an attractive ambient display that gave us information that might be relevant in our life.

Solution

Make a device that provides notifications with the help of its ambient display. The notifications provided are current popular movie genres around the local area. The display should be approachable and fun for everyone in the house even when not in use.

Process

Ideation

We wanted the design to look like a toy projector (Viewmaster) that closely resembled the "green one-eyed Monster Inc".

After spending countless hours designing the display, we decided on our final design.

Hardware

We used the following parts to create the Movie-Time :

  • Particle Photon - Microcontroller used to create Internet of Things devices.
  • Servo Motor – To move movie genre reel.
  • 3D Printed Cylinder - The is cylinder was 3D printed to make sure the Neopixel fit inside it.
  • Acrylic Sheets - It is used for the "toy" design of the box and the reel for projection.
  • Wood - It is used as the container for the electronic components.
  • Cotton Fabric - It is used as the main display and the movie genre is projected on it.

Final Prototype

It projects an image that resembles a genre for a movie that is coming up next (within 20mins). A neopixel is attached to the photon for the projection. The reel has been rastered with the laser cutter for image projection.

We have used a servo motor for rotating the reel to the genre of the movie at the required time. The neopixel remains turned off and the reel moves back to its base postion when the device is not in use.

We used the onconnect service from Gracenote that provides local movie show timings based on zipcode. Gracenote API link: http://developer.tmsapi.com/
